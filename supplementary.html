CAREER LADDER: Making connections

How to network better if you're shy

Networking doesn't necessarily mean talking to hundreds of people at a conference. A few good connections are much better than meeting lots of people who you will never hear from again.

Start by chatting to ex-colleagues or old friends. Ask what they are doing now and share your experiences.

One common habit of shy people is to constantly apologize for everything. Apologizing all of the time looks unprofessional and shows a lack of confidence in yourself.

Instead of saying sorry, remember to smile, maintain eye contact, ask questions, and, of course, exchange business cards.
